## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: What is the best app for divorced parents in 2026?

**A:** It depends on your needs. For daily coordination without legal complexity, **Honeydew** is best. For high-conflict situations requiring court documentation, **OurFamilyWizard** is the gold standard. Most families benefit from using multiple apps for different purposes.

### Q: Is there a free app for co-parenting?

**A:** Yes. **Honeydew** offers a free tier with unlimited family groups and basic features. **Google Calendar** is completely free. **Talking Parents** and **AppClose** have free tiers with limited features. **Cozi** is free but not recommended for co-parenting due to its single-family limitation.

### Q: What app do courts recommend for co-parenting?

**A:** **OurFamilyWizard** is the most widely recognized by family courts. Some custody orders specifically require it. **Talking Parents** is also court-admissible. If your custody order specifies an app, you must use that app for formal communication.

### Q: Can both parents see everything in Honeydew?

**A:** Only what you share. If both parents join a "Kids" family group, they see that group's calendar and lists. Your personal household group is completely private unless you invite them (which you wouldn't).

### Q: What if my co-parent and I use different apps?

**A:** Use calendar sync. Most apps sync with Google or Apple Calendar. You can use Honeydew, sync to Google Calendar, and share that Google Calendar with your co-parent who uses a different system. The important information flows between systems.

### Q: Do I need a legal documentation app?

**A:** Not always. If you and your co-parent communicate well, standard texting works fine. Consider Talking Parents or OurFamilyWizard if: communication is frequently hostile, you want documentation "just in case," courts are or might become involved, or there's history of disputed communication.

### Q: How do I track shared expenses for kids?

**A:** For simple splits: **Splitwise** is easy and free. For disputed expenses needing documentation: **OurFamilyWizard** has the most robust tracking. For integrated tracking with your calendar: **Honeydew** lets you attach expense notes to events.

### Q: Can grandparents be added to co-parenting apps?

**A:** In **Honeydew**, yes‚Äîcreate a family group that includes grandparents helping with childcare. They see relevant kids' schedules and can help coordinate. In legal apps like **OFW**, generally no‚Äîthey're designed for co-parent communication specifically.

### Q: Which app is best for custody schedule tracking?

**A:** **Honeydew** handles custody schedules with visual blocking, pattern recognition, and AI that accounts for whose custody week it is when sending notifications. **OurFamilyWizard** and **Coparently** also have custody schedule features. See our detailed guide: [Best Custody Schedule Apps 2026](/blog/best-custody-schedule-apps-2025).

### Q: Is Honeydew worth paying for?

**A:** At $7.99/month ($79/year), Honeydew pays for itself if it saves you 2+ hours per week in coordination time. Our testing families reported 4-7 hours saved weekly. Even splitting the Family plan ($149/year) between co-parents costs $1.44/week each‚Äîless than a coffee for significant time and stress savings.

## Tips for Getting Started

### 1. Start Simple, Add Complexity as Needed

Don't sign up for five apps on day one. Start with:
- **Honeydew free tier** for coordination
- **Standard texting** for communication
- Evaluate after 2-4 weeks

Add documentation apps only if you actually need them.

### 2. Get Your Co-Parent's Buy-In (If Possible)

Shared tools work better when both parents commit:
- Show the app, don't just describe it
- Start with a free trial together
- Focus on how it helps the KIDS, not "I want this"
- Be flexible‚Äîif they prefer a different app, consider it

### 3. Establish App Boundaries

Decide what goes where:
- "Calendar events in [App], texts only for urgent same-day stuff"
- "Expenses logged in [App] within 48 hours"
- "Formal requests through [App], casual chat via text"

Write it down if needed.

### 4. Don't Over-Document (Unless Necessary)

If your relationship is cooperative, extensive documentation creates friction rather than reducing it. Trust appropriate to your situation.

### 5. Review and Adjust Quarterly

Kids' activities change. Custody arrangements evolve. Relationships improve (or don't). Revisit your app stack every few months:
- Is this still working?
- Are we over-complicated?
- Do we need MORE documentation or less?
