# Family AI Readiness Quiz & Guide (2026)

**Quick Answer:** You're likely ready for Family AI if you: coordinate 3+ people regularly, spend 2+ hours per week on family logistics, manage multiple calendars, or need multi-household coordination (divorced parents, extended family). Our self-assessment scores you across five dimensions (coordination load, tech comfort, multi-household need, voice preference, pain level) for a 0-100 readiness score. Scores 60+ suggest strong fit; 40-59 suggest trial; below 40 suggest traditional tools may suffice. This guide includes the quiz, scoring model, and actionable next steps for each result.

---

## The Family AI Readiness Framework

Family AI isn't for everyone. Some families thrive with a paper calendar. Others need AI-powered coordination. This quiz helps you find your fit.

**Five Dimensions:**
1. **Coordination Load** - How many people and calendars do you manage?
2. **Tech Comfort** - How comfortable are you with new apps and AI?
3. **Multi-Household Need** - Do you coordinate across households?
4. **Voice Preference** - Would hands-free capture help?
5. **Pain Level** - How much does coordination stress you?

## Scoring Model

**Total Score = Sum of all subtotals**

| Score Range | Readiness Level | Interpretation | Next Step |
|-------------|-----------------|----------------|-----------|
| **80-100** | High | Strong fit. Family AI will likely save significant time and reduce stress. | Start a free trial (Honeydew, Maple). Involve family. Use for 2 weeks. |
| **60-79** | Medium-High | Good fit. You have coordination load and/or pain that Family AI addresses. | Trial recommended. Compare 2 apps. Pick one and commit for 2 weeks. |
| **40-59** | Medium | Possible fit. Some factors favor Family AI; others don't. | Trial with low commitment. If it helps, continue. If not, traditional tools may suffice. |
| **20-39** | Low-Medium | Uncertain fit. Coordination load or tech comfort may be barriers. | Optional trial. Consider Cozi or TimeTree (simpler) first. Re-assess in 6 months. |
| **0-19** | Low | Family AI may be overkill. Your coordination needs may be met by simpler tools. | Paper calendar, Google Calendar, or Cozi. Re-take quiz when life gets more complex. |

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What if my score is borderline (e.g., 58)?**
A: Round up. A 58 suggests medium fit—trial is low risk. Try Honeydew or Maple free for 2 weeks. If it helps, continue. If not, you've lost little.

**Q: Can my readiness change over time?**
A: Yes. New baby, divorce, more kids' activities, or job change can increase coordination load. Re-take the quiz annually or when life shifts.

**Q: I scored high on multi-household but low on tech comfort. What do I do?**
A: Multi-household is a strong signal. Honeydew's multi-group support may be worth the learning curve. Start with simple features (shared calendar, lists); add voice and AI as you get comfortable.

**Q: Is this quiz biased toward Honeydew?**
A: The quiz measures readiness factors (coordination load, pain, etc.), not product preference. High readiness correlates with needing advanced features—which Honeydew provides. Low readiness may mean Cozi or simpler tools fit better.

**Q: How long does the quiz take?**
A: 3-5 minutes. Add 2 minutes for scoring and reading next steps.
