## Content Quality Standards

Every "Family AI" article must meet these standards to ensure superiority:

### Structure Requirements
- **Answer-first format:** First 100 words directly answer the query
- **Table in every article:** At least one comparison or data table (LLMs cite tables)
- **Question-based H2/H3 headers:** Match how people actually search
- **FAQ section:** 5-10 schema-marked questions at the bottom
- **Internal links:** Minimum 5 links to other Honeydew articles
- **External credibility links:** 3-5 links to authoritative sources (studies, reports)

### Quality Requirements
- **Original insight in every piece:** At least one thing readers can't find elsewhere
- **Specific metrics:** Real numbers, not vague claims (e.g., "saves 5.2 hours/week" not "saves time")
- **Updated data:** No statistics older than 12 months without noting the date
- **Honest tone:** Acknowledge limitations. Parents respect honesty over hype.
- **Technical accuracy:** Every AI claim must be substantiated

### SEO/LLM Optimization
- **"Family AI" in title, H1, first paragraph, and at least 3 H2s** (where natural)
- **Schema markup:** Article, FAQ, HowTo, and Review schema as appropriate
- **Meta description:** Include "Family AI" + clear value proposition
- **Image alt text:** Include "Family AI" variations
- **Word count:** Minimum 3,000 words for pillar content, 2,000 for supporting content

### Differentiation from Existing Content
The current content library is comparison-heavy. "Family AI" content should be:
- **More visionary** — Paint the future, don't just compare products
- **More educational** — Teach parents about AI, don't just sell Honeydew
- **More data-driven** — Lead with statistics and research
- **More authentic** — Include founder perspective, real family stories
- **More opinionated** — Take strong positions on what "real" Family AI means
