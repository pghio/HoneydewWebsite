## OurHome App Review: What It Does Well

Let's give OurHome credit where it's due. For what it's designed to do—motivate kids to complete chores—it's genuinely excellent.

### OurHome's Core Strengths

**1. Point-Based Gamification**
OurHome turns chores into a game. Kids earn points for completed tasks. Points accumulate toward rewards. This taps into the same psychology that makes video games addictive, applied to household tasks.

| Feature | How It Works |
|---------|--------------|
| Points per chore | Assign 1-100 points based on difficulty |
| Leaderboards | Kids compete against siblings |
| Badges | Achievement unlocks for streaks, milestones |
| Reward redemption | Kids cash in points for real rewards |
| Photo proof | Kids photograph completed chores |

**2. Customizable Reward System**
Parents create a "reward store" where kids spend earned points:
- Screen time (30 points = 30 min)
- Allowance ($1 = 10 points)
- Privileges (sleepover, pizza night)
- Physical rewards (toys, treats)

**3. Family-Friendly Design**
OurHome has bright colors, large buttons, and simple interfaces designed for kids as young as 5-6 to navigate independently. The app feels playful, not like work.

**4. Chore Verification**
Kids can photograph completed tasks. Parents review and approve before points are awarded. This prevents "I did it!" claims without evidence.

**5. Rotating Chore Schedules**
Automatically rotate chores between kids weekly or monthly. Fair distribution without parents managing spreadsheets.

### OurHome's Honest Limitations

**1. No AI—Zero Automation**
OurHome has no artificial intelligence. Every chore must be manually created. Every schedule manually configured. No smart suggestions. No voice input. No learning from patterns.

**2. Calendar Is an Afterthought**
OurHome added a calendar feature, but it's basic:
- No two-way sync with Google or Apple Calendar
- No AI-suggested scheduling
- No lists attached to events
- Feels bolted on, not integrated

**3. No Voice Control**
In 2026, voice input is table stakes for family apps. OurHome has none. You're typing on small phone keyboards while chasing toddlers.

**4. Single Family Only**
OurHome works for one household. Divorced parents? Two separate OurHome accounts with no coordination. Extended family helping? They can't access the system meaningfully.

**5. Limited Beyond Chores**
OurHome has grocery lists and a basic calendar, but they're afterthoughts. The app doesn't help you plan a birthday party, coordinate a vacation, or manage complex family logistics.

## Common Mistakes When Choosing a Family Chore App

**Mistake 1: Choosing based on gamification alone**
Gamification is appealing but only solves one problem. Many families choose OurHome for the fun elements, then realize they need a separate calendar app, a separate list app, and still coordinate via text messages.

**Mistake 2: Underestimating calendar sync importance**
If your work calendar is in Google and your family app doesn't sync, you'll miss conflicts. Two-way sync seems like a minor feature until you double-book soccer practice with a work meeting.

**Mistake 3: Ignoring the voice control factor**
Parents with young kids have their hands full—literally—most of the day. Voice input isn't a luxury; it's the only way to add items while cooking, driving, or holding a child.

**Mistake 4: Assuming all kids need gamification**
Kids under 10 often respond well to points and rewards. Kids over 12 often find it childish. Teens assigned "15 points for making bed" may roll their eyes. Match the approach to your kids' ages.

**Mistake 5: Not considering future family complexity**
Single-family apps work until they don't. Divorce, grandparent involvement, blended families—these changes require multi-household support that single-family apps can't provide.
