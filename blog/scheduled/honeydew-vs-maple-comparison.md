## The Philosophy Difference: Folders vs. Assistant

Both apps claim to solve the same problem: the crushing weight of family mental load. But they approach it from opposite directions.

**Maple's approach is "Structure":**
Maple organizes your life into "Topics" (folders). You have a folder for "Trips," a folder for "Medical," a folder for "Chores." Inside each, you have chat, lists, and calendar events. It's remarkably similar to Slack or Microsoft Teams, but for families. It assumes the solution to chaos is **better filing**.

**Honeydew's approach is "Intelligence":**
Honeydew assumes you don't want to manage folders—you want things done. Instead of creating a "Dinner" folder, you just say "Plan dinner for Tuesday." Honeydew is an **agent**, not a filing cabinet. It assumes the solution to chaos is **active help**.

## Feature Breakdown

### 1. Voice & Natural Language
**Honeydew:** Built on Whisper AI. You can ramble for 45 seconds about a school trip, groceries, and a dentist appointment, and it will parse everything into the right places instantly. It's designed for "hands-full" parenting.
**Maple:** Relies mostly on typing and tapping. You navigate to a topic, then create an item. It feels more like administrative work.

### 2. AI Capabilities
**Honeydew:**
* "Plan our weekend" → Generates itinerary, packing lists, and reminders.
* "We're out of milk" → Adds to grocery list contextually.
* Proactive: "You have a free evening Thursday, want to tackle that project?"

**Maple:**
* Uses AI mostly for "Magic Tools" (like a meal planner), but it feels like a separate feature you invoke, not an integrated brain that knows your schedule.

### 3. The "Marketplace" (Maple's Unique Feature)
**Maple** has a built-in marketplace to hire people (house cleaners, landscapers, etc.). If your version of solving mental load is *outsourcing to a human*, Maple is superior. Honeydew focuses on solving the coordination problem itself, not hiring gig workers.

## Which One Is For You?

### Choose Maple if:
* You love categorizing things into folders.
* You treat your family like a business (Slack-style interface).
* You frequently look to hire local help for tasks.
* You want a "shared inbox" feel.

### Choose Honeydew if:
* You hate navigating menus and just want to "say it and forget it."
* You want AI to actively plan for you, not just store your plans.
* You value speed (Voice AI) over structure.
* You want the "Assistant" experience, not the "Manager" experience.
