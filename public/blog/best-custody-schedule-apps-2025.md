## Why Custody Scheduling Is So Hard

Managing a custody schedule sounds simple: Mom has the kids these days, Dad has them these days. Done.

Except it's never that simple:

- **School events** that fall on the "wrong" parent's day
- **Sports schedules** that span both households
- **Medical appointments** requiring coordination
- **Last-minute changes** ("Can you take them this weekend instead?")
- **Holiday shuffling** that changes every year
- **Activity pickups** that require handoff logistics
- **Summer schedules** that differ from school-year patterns

And underlying all of this: **communication with your ex** that may range from seamless to incredibly difficult.

The right custody schedule app handles the logistics AND the communication challenges of your specific situation.

## Common Custody Schedule Patterns (How to Set Up)

### Week-On/Week-Off (50/50)

**Pattern:** 7 days Mom, 7 days Dad, repeat
**Setup:** Create recurring 14-day event blocks, alternating parents
**Best for:** Older children who handle transitions well

### 2-2-3 (50/50)

**Pattern:** 
- Week 1: Mom Mon-Tue, Dad Wed-Thu, Mom Fri-Sun
- Week 2: Dad Mon-Tue, Mom Wed-Thu, Dad Fri-Sun
**Setup:** Create weekly pattern with specific day assignments
**Best for:** Young children who need more frequent contact with both parents

### 5-2-2-5 (50/50)

**Pattern:** Mom has Mon-Fri (5), Dad has Sat-Sun (2), Dad has Mon-Tue (2), Mom has Wed-Sun (5)
**Setup:** Complex pattern requiring careful calendar blocking
**Best for:** Parents wanting consistent weekday routines

### Every Other Weekend

**Pattern:** Primary parent weekdays, non-custodial parent every other weekend
**Setup:** Recurring bi-weekly weekend events
**Best for:** Traditional arrangements where one parent is primary

### Right of First Refusal

**Pattern:** If custodial parent can't care for child, other parent gets first option
**Setup:** In Honeydew, use shared task lists for availability; in OFW, document through messages
**Best for:** Maximizing both parents' time with children

## Tips for Custody Schedule Success

### 1. Enter the Full Pattern in Advance

Don't add custody events day by day. Enter the entire year's pattern at once:
- Less room for error
- Visual pattern helps planning
- Both parents can see far ahead

### 2. Add Activities to the Shared Calendar Immediately

When kids sign up for anything, add it to shared calendar instantly:
- Soccer registration ‚Üí add all games and practices
- School calendar released ‚Üí add all events
- Birthday party invite ‚Üí add immediately

### 3. Use Color-Coding Consistently

Agree on colors with co-parent:
- Blue = Dad's custody
- Pink = Mom's custody  
- Green = Shared events (both attend)
- Yellow = Kids' activities (regardless of custody)

### 4. Create "Handoff" Events

Add specific handoff logistics:
- Time of exchange
- Location (school pickup, home, neutral location)
- Who picks up vs drops off
- What items need to transfer (backpack, medication, gear)

### 5. Document Schedule Changes

When schedules change:
- In cooperative relationships: Update calendar, send notification
- In contentious relationships: Document in OFW before changing calendar
