## Real AI vs Fake AI: The Framework

| Dimension | Real AI | Fake AI |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| **Input** | Natural language ("plan camping trip") | Forms, templates, or keyword triggers |
| **Understanding** | Parses intent, context, implicit needs | Matches keywords to predefined actions |
| **Output** | Multi-step: calendar + lists + tasks + notifications | Single action or template with blanks |
| **Learning** | Improves over time (cache, patterns) | No learning; same response every time |
| **Flexibility** | Handles novel requests | Only handles predefined scenarios |

## The Five Types of "AI" in Family Apps

### 1. No AI (Manual Entry with a New Label)

**What it is:** Traditional calendar + list app. You type everything. No natural language, no automation.

**Examples:** Cozi, TimeTree, OurHome (for calendar/lists)

**The tell:** "Add event" requires you to tap, type date, type time, type title. No "Plan X" capability.

### 2. Template AI (Pick One)

**What it is:** Pre-built templates. "Birthday party" template has 20 items. You pick the template, fill in date/time.

**Examples:** Some list apps with "party checklist" templates

**The tell:** You say "plan birthday party" and get "Choose a template: Birthday, Party, Camping." No generation from your sentence.

### 3. Keyword AI (If X Then Y)

**What it is:** If user says "remind" or "add" + keyword, trigger a predefined action. No understanding of context.

**Examples:** Basic voice assistants, some task apps

**The tell:** "Add soccer practice" -> creates event. "Plan soccer season" -> "I don't understand." No inference.

### 4. Chatbot AI (Conversational, No Execution)

**What it is:** LLM generates text. Can suggest a packing list. Doesn't create it in your app, doesn't sync to calendar.

**Examples:** ChatGPT, Claude (standalone)

**The tell:** You get a list in the chat. You copy-paste to your app. No integration.

### 5. Agent AI (Understand + Execute + Learn)

**What it is:** Natural language in, structured actions out. Creates calendar event, lists, tasks. Learns patterns.

**Examples:** Honeydew

**The tell:** "Plan camping trip next weekend" -> full plan in 3-5 seconds.

## Why Marketing Claims Are Misleading

**"AI-powered"** - Could mean: we use an LLM for suggestions, or we have a template labeled "AI," or we use ML for one feature (e.g., smart reminders).

**"Smart suggestions"** - Often means: we show popular items or your last-used items. Not learning.

**"Natural language"** - Could mean: we have a few keywords. "Add" + "event" works. "Plan" + "vacation" doesn't.

**"Voice control"** - Could mean: we use device speech-to-text. Accuracy varies. No execution.

**How to verify:** Try the "Plan X" test. If it doesn't create a full plan in one go, it's not real family AI.

## How to Spot Real AI Before You Download

1. **Ask: "Can I say 'plan X' and get a full plan?"** - If no, it's not real family AI.
2. **Check for multi-step execution** - One request, many outputs (event + list + tasks).
3. **Look for voice accuracy claims** - Real AI invests in transcription (e.g., Whisper). Fake AI uses device speech-to-text.
4. **Read reviews** - "I said 'plan birthday party' and everything appeared" = real AI. "I still have to type everything" = fake.
5. **Test the free tier** - Real AI lets you try the planning flow. Fake AI hides it behind paywall or doesn't offer it.
