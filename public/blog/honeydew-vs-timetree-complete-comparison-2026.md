## What is TimeTree?

TimeTree launched in 2014 from Tokyo-based JUBILEE WORKS and has grown to over 60 million users across 170+ countries. It pioneered the concept of a "shared calendar for couples and families" with a clean, intuitive interface that focused purely on schedule visibility.

### TimeTree's Core Features

**Shared Calendars:** Create multiple shared calendars for different groups (family, work team, sports club). Everyone with access sees all events.

**Color-Coded Members:** Each person gets a unique color, making it easy to see who has what scheduled at a glance.

**Memo Function:** Attach notes and images to calendar events. TimeTree was early to recognize that events often need context.

**Keep Me Posted:** A social feed showing recent updates so you never miss when someone adds or changes an event.

**Two-Way Sync:** Connects with Google Calendar, Apple Calendar, and Outlook so events flow between your personal calendar and shared TimeTree calendars.

**Free Tier:** TimeTree's free version is genuinely usable—most features work without payment. Premium ($4.49/month) removes ads, adds unlimited photo storage, and provides event history.

### TimeTree's Strengths

1. **Completely Free:** The free tier is more generous than most competitors. You can share calendars with unlimited members without paying.

2. **Simplicity:** TimeTree does one thing well. There's no learning curve. Add events, share the calendar, done.

3. **Polished Mobile Experience:** The app is fast, clean, and reliable. Japanese software engineering at its best.

4. **Social Features:** The "Keep Me Posted" feed and memo function add light social elements that families enjoy.

5. **Broad Platform Support:** iOS, Android, and web apps ensure everyone can access the calendar regardless of device.

### TimeTree's Limitations

1. **No Lists:** TimeTree is calendar-only. You can't create shopping lists, packing lists, or to-do lists. Events exist in isolation.

2. **No AI:** There's no intelligent assistant. Every event requires manual entry.

3. **No Voice Input:** You must type everything. No hands-free operation.

4. **No Multi-Family Architecture:** Creating separate groups for co-parents, extended family, or multiple households requires creating entirely separate calendars and manually managing membership.

5. **No Task Assignment:** You can see who created an event, but you can't assign tasks to family members or track completion.

6. **Ads in Free Tier:** The free version includes advertisements.
