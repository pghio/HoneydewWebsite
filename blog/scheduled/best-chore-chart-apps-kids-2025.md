Every parent wants to:
- Teach kids responsibility
- Get help around the house
- Not nag constantly
- Maybe connect chores to allowance

**The challenge:** Paper chore charts fail (inconsistent, easy to ignore). But what digital approach works?

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Should chores be connected to allowance?**
A: Philosophical debate! Some say chores are family contribution (no pay). Others say it teaches financial connection. Both approaches can work. Choose based on your values.

**Q: What age should kids start using chore apps?**
A: Gamified apps (S'moresUp, ChoreMonster): 4-5 years old. Integrated apps (Honeydew, OurHome): 7-8+ when they can navigate independently.

**Q: How do I get kids to actually use the app?**
A: Gamification helps younger kids. Older kids respond to: clear expectations, consistent consequences, choice in what chores they do, and seeing parents use the same organizational tools.

**Q: Can siblings compete for chores in Honeydew?**
A: Not with points/leaderboards. But you can assign rotating chores and track completion. For competition, OurHome is better.

**Q: What if my spouse thinks chore apps are unnecessary?**
A: Start with Honeydew's integrated approachâ€”it's a family calendar that happens to track tasks. Less "another app" resistance.
